About us

Rose Land Primary School, West Bengal, India was established by Ajita Toppo in the year 1997, now it is providing education to 130 children of the poor tea plantation workers children in a remote village named Nahartaka from kindergarten(KG) to class IV. Ajita Toppo belongs from the tribal community of North Bengal has been working tirelessly for the past 30 years to bring education to her village. Ajita Toppo used to teach the children of poor tea garden workers, free of cost. Ajita Toppo recalls "See, when I started, there wasn’t a single school in this village. This was back in 1988. I thought, if I wait for someone else to open a school, I’d have to sit waiting forever. I didn’t have any facilities, not even a simple blackboard back then. But despite that, the number of children kept increasing. I don’t own my house, we rent this place. I was teaching children in my living room, but when the numbers started rising it wasn’t feasible. So I rented a place in the village and started conducting the classes there. These children come from destitute families, who wouldn’t have been able to afford high amount for fees, so I started taking only Rs 20 from them."

Although hailing from poor family she was educated and loved to teach and help others. Gradually many students, got attracted by her amazing style of teaching and she didn't charge any money for teaching. To make sitting arrangement for increasing number of students, she had to change her teaching place five times. When number of students reached more than 70, she called all guardians of the students and decided to start a school. A school committee was made, she managed to find a rented house to run the primary school (kindergarten to class 4). She started taking only 20 rupees from every student to run the school.

The number of students started increasing further. The rented house was not sufficient to accommodate all the students. In 2014 a local person, Sunil Kujur, donated a piece of land for the school. After one year “Faith in God” mission helped to raise a school building in that land.

A local NGO Maha Jiban has donated learning material to her school like Lego building blocks, an iPad, Wi-Fi connection, abacus as well as some stationery.

The fees of the school is still very less (monthly rupees 150). Ajita Toppo, her daughter Jaya Toppo and few young people from that area are working and teaching for the school.

As all the 140 students , who study there, belongs to very poor family . The school fee is not sufficient to meet the need of the school.

The problem they are facing now are:

1.Unable to build 4 toilets for teachers and students.

2.Unable to pay the sufficient wages to teachers.

3.Unable to arrange school vehicle and give school boundaries.

4.Unable to perform all school activities .

5.Unable to buy a computer and projector to display educational content from internet.
